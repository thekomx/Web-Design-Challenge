Vs. Temperature

  This scatter plot shows temperature of more than 600 samples cities around the earth, Southern and Northern Hemisphere. Each dot represents a sample city.


  Higher position of a dot in the graph means higher temperature (warmer weather) in degree Fahrenheit.


  Left hand side of the graph represents the Southern Hemisphere (starts from 60 degree south). Dots located further to the right of the graph represent cities lacated further to the North of the earth (ends at 80 degree north).


  From the both bottom coners left and right of the graph, dots (cities) are located higher toward middle of the graph. This means cities further away from the Southern and Northern Hemisphere toward the Equator have higher temperature.


  However, from the left side of the graph beyond the Equator (0 degree) trends still keep going higher. On the other hand, from the right side of the graph, the trend has peak and start getting lower at around 20-40 degree north.


  From this dataset the graph shows that the futher away a city from the Nothern and Southern Hemisphere toward 20-40 degree north, the higher temperature it will get. NOT the Equator as first thought.
